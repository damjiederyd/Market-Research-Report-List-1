<p><p>Algae biofuel technologies encompass various cultivation systems: Open cultivation systems utilize natural sunlight and water bodies, promoting cost efficiency but facing contamination risks. Closed cultivation systems consist of controlled environments, like photobioreactors, which enhance productivity and minimize contamination but are more expensive to build and maintain. Sea-based cultivation systems leverage marine environments, allowing for large-scale growth without competing for arable land, though they face challenges such as water quality and access to ocean locations. Each system offers unique advantages and drawbacks.</p></p>
