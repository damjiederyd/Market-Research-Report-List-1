<p><p>The oral controlled release drug delivery technology market encompasses various systems designed to regulate the release of medication over time. Diffusion-controlled systems rely on the gradual movement of the drug through a polymer matrix. Dissolution-controlled systems enable the drug to release based on the dissolution rate within the gastrointestinal tract. Osmotically controlled systems utilize osmotic pressure to facilitate drug release. Combined dissolution and diffusion systems leverage both mechanisms. Ion exchange resins modulate drug release through ionic interactions, while hydro-dynamically balanced systems maintain buoyancy, ensuring extended release. Other specialized methods also exist within this market.</p></p>
