<p><p>The antimicrobial medical textiles market features two primary types: non-woven fabrics and woven fabrics. Non-woven fabrics are engineered from fibers that are bonded together through mechanical, thermal, or chemical processes, offering advantages like lightweight, breathability, and efficient barrier properties. They are often used in applications like surgical gowns and masks. In contrast, woven fabrics are created by interlacing threads and provide durability and strength, making them suitable for long-lasting medical textiles like bed linens and uniforms. Both types enhance infection control in healthcare settings.</p></p>
